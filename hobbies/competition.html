My Journey Through AI/ML/DL Competitions

Date: August 2024

Participating in machine learning competitions has been one of the most rewarding experiences of my career. The challenges, the learning opportunities, and the sense of community have all contributed to my growth as a data scientist. Over the years, I have participated in different AI/ML/DL competitions across various platforms, each offering unique challenges and experiences. Here's a look at the platforms I've competed on and the insights I've gained from each one.

1. Kaggle

Kaggle, the largest and most popular platform, has been a staple in my competition journey. With over 10 million users, Kaggle offers a vast array of competitions, datasets, and community-driven discussions. My participation here has allowed me to engage with a global community and tackle some of the most challenging data science problems. Profile: https://www.kaggle.com/bhaskarjyotidutta

2. DrivenData

DrivenData focuses on social impact competitions, and my involvement in their challenges has been incredibly fulfilling. The detailed write-ups and research reports following each competition have provided deep insights into solving real-world problems with data science.

3. AIcrowd

AIcrowd is known for its research-oriented competitions, often collaborating with top institutions like NeurIPS. Competing on AIcrowd has pushed me to explore cutting-edge techniques and apply them to real-world problems.

4. Zindi

Zindi, with its focus on Africa, has introduced me to unique datasets and challenges that are less common on other platforms. Participating in Zindi competitions has broadened my understanding of global data science challenges.

5. CodaLab

CodaLab's open-source nature has made it a go-to platform for a wide range of competitions. I've enjoyed the flexibility and variety of challenges hosted here, and the opportunity to contribute to open-source projects.

6. Tianchi

As part of Alibaba Cloud, Tianchi has offered some of the most competitive challenges with significant prize pools. Competing on Tianchi has helped me refine my skills and compete against top global talent.

7. Signate

Signate is the largest Japanese-language ML competition platform, and participating here has given me insight into the unique approaches and methodologies prevalent in Japan's data science community.

8. EvalAI

EvalAI, part of CloudCV, has offered a diverse set of challenges, allowing me to test my skills in different areas of AI and machine learning.

9. Waymo Open Dataset Challenges

Competing in the Waymo Open Dataset Challenges has been a fascinating experience, particularly with the focus on autonomous driving. The high-quality datasets and the competitive environment have been instrumental in advancing my expertise in computer vision.
